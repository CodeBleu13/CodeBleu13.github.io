Bleu Game Awards

Welcome to the Bleu Game Awards where the players make the decisions and the player vote is 100% of the final score. The idea for the BGAs came from watching game award shows and just not agreeing with either the nominees or the final vote. Occasionally, the chosen games seemed a little sus, especially compared to the games that came out and the way they were rated upon release verses how they were recieved by the time the Awards Show came around. This lead to digging in to the background of learning about the Game Awards and how nominees are chosen and voted on. As it turns out, nominations and primary voting is done by a pannel of media judges who are picked by the big companies (Microsoft, Sony, EA, Ubisoft, etc.), basically, people who are paid to play these games. "Fan" voting only comprises 10% of the final vote, basically relegating popular opinion to tie breaking. So, in response, the Bleu Game Awards were born, with the first award show on December 12, 2020. In the BGAs, nominations and voting is all fan based. Each vote is weighed the same and all analysis and data is open and available to anyone wishing to peruse it.

BGA Rules

General:

  1. Games must come out between November 1 of the previous year to Oct 31 of the current year.
  2. Nominations take place during the month of November, the deadline being November 30.
  3. Voting takes place December 1-10 (allowing 2 days for results to be calculated) NOTE Voting can be extended to the 11th.
  4. There is a category of backlog, or, as they are refered to in the BGAs, Quinquo Games, with the following rules:
    1. The game had to be played or watched within the last 2 years.
    2. The game's release date must be before November 1 of the previous year.

Nominations

  1. Each nominator can select up to 4 games for each category.
  2. When all nominations are in, the top 6 (more if including ties of >2 nominees) in each category will be selected for voting, to a maximum of 10.
  3. Tie-breaking will be based on the following in order::
    1. Metacritic score
    2. Number of platforms available
    3. Sales
  4. Nominations will be anonymous.
  5. Nomination results will be released concurrently with voting options.

Voting:

  1. Voting will be ranked voting, using Instant Runoff Voting (IRV):
    • For each category, Voters get one (1) vote. They then rank the nominees.
    • The nominee with the fewest votes is eliminated and votes are transfered to the next choice.
    • process repeats until one (1) candidate wins a majority.
  2. Voting will be anonymous.
  3. Voting results will be released during and/or after the Award Ceremony.
